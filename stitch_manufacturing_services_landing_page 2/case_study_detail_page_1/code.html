High-Performance Automotive Bracket

A case study on precision engineering and cost-effective manufacturing for the automotive industry.

Project Overview

Client

AutoInnovate Corp.

Industry

Automotive

Services Used

CNC Machining, Anodizing

Key Result

30% Cost Reduction

The Challenge

AutoInnovate Corp. approached us with a critical need: to re-engineer a key engine mounting bracket for their new line of performance vehicles. The existing part was prone to stress fractures under high-torque conditions and was too heavy, negatively impacting the vehicle's overall weight and fuel efficiency. They required a stronger, lighter, and more cost-effective solution without compromising on safety or performance.

Our Solution

Our team of engineers and machinists developed a multi-faceted solution. We started with a comprehensive Finite Element Analysis (FEA) to identify stress points in the original design. Using this data, we redesigned the bracket in CAD, incorporating a lattice structure to reduce material usage while enhancing structural integrity. The part was then manufactured using our 5-axis CNC mills from a single block of aerospace-grade 7075 aluminum, followed by a Type III hardcoat anodizing for maximum durability and corrosion resistance.

The Outcome

The new bracket exceeded all of the client's performance targets. It was 40% lighter and 150% stronger than the original part. Our optimized manufacturing process also led to a 30% reduction in per-unit cost, delivering significant savings at scale. The project was completed two weeks ahead of schedule, allowing AutoInnovate to accelerate their production timeline.
